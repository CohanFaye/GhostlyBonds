Do You Have a Haunting?

This walkthrough is designed to help you determine whether your space might be experiencing paranormal activity. Follow each step carefully and document your findings thoroughly.

Step 1 – Initial Thoughts

  • Take a Holistic View: Consider your emotional state, familiarity with the space, and any known history of paranormal activity.
  • Reconstruct the Event: Recall the moment in vivid detail. Specifics help clarify what may have occurred.
  • Keep a Detailed Log: Track unusual activity for at least four weeks. Include sounds, smells, object movement, and emotional impressions.

Step 2 – Recurring Phenomena

  • Sudden Temperature Drops: Monitor cold zones and seal off drafts. Record fluctuations over time.
  • Phantom Footsteps: Rule out pets or plumbing. Use motion-sensitive audio cameras if needed.
  • Animal Reactions: Log odd pet behavior after ruling out medical causes.
  • Visitor Sensitivity: Note guest impressions — discomfort may indicate a pattern.
  • Mysterious Scents: Track unexplained smells like sulfur or perfume. Note time and location.
  • Children’s Perceptions: Listen to recurring themes in their descriptions. Seek help if distressing.
  • Unusual Door/Window Movement: Inspect for drafts. If unexplained, monitor for further anomalies.
  • Displaced Objects: Use trackers or cameras to monitor movement. Document thoroughly.
  • Unexplained Sounds: Investigate pipes, wildlife, or settling wood. If persistent, seek expert help.
  • Orb Sightings: Avoid flash photography. Observe motion — erratic flickers suggest dust, slow movement may not.
  • Pareidolia: Assess lighting and image quality before assuming faces in shadows are paranormal.
  • EVP: Use voice-activated devices to ask questions. Review playback for anomalies.
  • Apparition Encounters: Rule out illusions. If threatening, document and seek professional guidance.

Step 3 – Be Cautious

  • Proceed with Care: Treat the situation with seriousness and respect. Avoid engaging directly with unknown forces unless you have deep knowledge and experience.
  • Stay Clear-Minded: Never attempt contact while under the influence. Clarity is essential for safety and credibility.
  • Respect Boundaries: Acknowledging spiritual entities may escalate activity. Prioritize the well-being of those affected and avoid communication unless guided by professionals.

Step 4 – Submit Your Documentation

  • Submit Your Evidence: Provide specific details, including any video, photo, or audio recordings.
  • Written Accounts: If no media is available, submit a journal-style record with dates, times, and descriptions.
  • Be Truthful: We take every report seriously. Accuracy helps us assess your case with care and respect.
